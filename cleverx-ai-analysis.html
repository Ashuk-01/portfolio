Designing AI-Powered Research Analysis

Research teams across the industry face the same bottleneck — too much time spent analysing, not enough time building. Product teams and researchers spend days reviewing recordings, pulling out insights, and compiling reports before they can act on anything. The analysis phase was taking longer than the research itself.

CleverX's goal was to solve this: help researchers analyse large volumes of interviews faster using AI — summaries, highlights, insights, transcripts — so teams could get to decisions and start building sooner.

I led the research and design for this feature, working with the team to take it from competitive analysis to shipped product.

Role UI/UX Designer & Design Engineer
Tools Figma
Year 2025

Context

The Problem

The manual analysis workflow was the biggest time sink in the entire research process:

  • After every moderated interview, researchers had to rewatch full recordings to find the moments that mattered — a single 30-minute interview could take over an hour to process
  • Identifying patterns meant jumping between multiple interview transcripts, manually tagging quotes, and trying to hold context across dozens of responses
  • Compiling shareable reports meant copying quotes into documents, writing summaries from scratch, and formatting everything for stakeholders who just wanted the bottom line
  • The longer analysis took, the longer product teams waited to act — and in fast-moving companies, that delay costs real momentum

The core UX challenge was clear: how do you compress hours of manual cognitive work into something a researcher can review, trust, and share in minutes — without losing the nuance that makes qualitative research valuable?

Research

Competitive Analysis

Before designing anything, I needed to understand how this problem was being solved across the industry.

  • Maze, Lyssna, UserTesting, User Interviews — Studied how each platform handles AI transcription, summarisation, and report generation for moderated and unmoderated studies
  • Used the findings to understand what patterns existed across platforms and what approach would work best for CleverX's researchers

This research shaped every design decision that followed.

Thinking

Design Decisions

The biggest challenge wasn't what features to build — it was how to present AI-generated analysis in a way researchers would actually trust and use.

  • AI alongside source, not instead of — Every AI summary sits next to the original transcript and quotes. Researchers can always verify what the AI is telling them. This was a deliberate choice — we didn't want researchers to blindly accept AI output
  • Interview and project-level summaries — Each interview gets its own AI summary with representative quotes. At the project level, a combined summary pulls together findings across all interviews — giving researchers both the detail and the big picture depending on what they need
  • Highlights as a first-class element — Key moments and quotes are pulled out and presented as standalone, quotable pieces. These become the building blocks for reports and stakeholder presentations
  • Export that's actually useful — PDF reports and CSV exports designed to be stakeholder-ready. The goal was: a researcher should be able to go from "study complete" to "sharing findings with the team" in one click

Deliverables

What I Designed

The end-to-end AI analysis experience for moderated interviews:

  • AI transcripts — Automatic transcription of interview recordings with searchable, scannable text
  • AI summaries — Interview-level and project-level summaries with representative quotes
  • Highlights — Key moments and quotes automatically identified and ready to reference
  • Insight generation — Structured findings generated from raw responses
  • Export and share — PDF reports and CSV exports, shareable with stakeholders in one click

Impact

What Changed

  • Shipped the AI analysis feature for moderated interviews on CleverX
  • Transformed a process that took researchers days into one that takes minutes — from raw interviews to shareable findings
  • Gave researchers AI they could trust by always connecting summaries back to source quotes and transcripts
  • Helped product teams act faster on research — less time waiting for analysis, more time building
  • The feature remains part of CleverX's core product today
