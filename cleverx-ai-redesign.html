Redesigning CleverX with AI

CleverX is an AI-powered user research platform that helps companies design, recruit, conduct, and analyze research studies across B2B and B2C audiences. I've been a UI/UX Designer here for over 5 years.

Role UI/UX Designer & Design Engineer
Tools Claude Code, Git, Figma
Year 2025–2026

The Shift

In the last few months, my workflow changed completely. I went from designing in Figma and handing off to developers, to writing prompts in Claude Code, making design decisions in the browser, and pushing code to staging through Git. Bugs I'd normally flag and wait days for? Fixed and shipped the same cycle.

This project covers two pieces of work from that shift: a redesign of the participant-side experience and fixes across the researcher study dashboard — designed, coded, and shipped using Claude Code.

Part 1

Participant Side Redesign

The Problems

After running audits and walking through the flows myself, a lot of issues surfaced:

  • Project cards were missing relevant badges — participants had no quick way to know a project's state, urgency, or expiry without clicking into each one
  • Study details pages showed various states but with missing context — no clarity on what happens next, when to expect payment, or what the participant should do in the meantime
  • The cards had poor information hierarchy — key details were buried while less relevant content took up visual space, making it hard for participants to scan and take action quickly
  • Study detail pages across all states — action required, paid, awaiting incentive, expired — had center-aligned content that made scanning difficult, no clear visual hierarchy, and missing navigation
  • A mix of old and new design system components made the experience feel inconsistent

What I Shipped

  • Project cards — Fixed the UX and information placement. Researched how other platforms handle similar cards, then restructured ours — surfacing the details participants actually need and adding relevant status badges
  • Study details page — The biggest overhaul. Added next steps, payment timelines, and clear expectations for every state. Participants should never have to guess what happens after they complete a study
  • All study states — Redesigned detail pages for every state. Fixed the center-aligned layout, built proper visual hierarchy, added navigation, and filled in missing information
  • Consistency pass — Swapped all old components for the current design system across every page
  • Responsive — Every redesigned page was built responsive from the start, not retrofitted after

Part 2

Researcher Study Dashboard

The Problems

The researcher side had issues that directly slowed down their core workflow — verifying participants and managing studies:

  • Researchers couldn't efficiently verify participants because the drawer was missing critical information — skills, work experience, industries, and qualification details were either absent or dumped with no structure. This meant researchers had to dig through scattered data to make approval decisions
  • The overview page made scanning difficult — repeated information and duplicate buttons created confusion instead of clarity
  • Study controls were confusing — pause buttons didn't clearly explain what they controlled, making it unclear what action a researcher was actually taking
  • Key stats showed numbers but with no way to navigate and check the relevant participants — researchers could see counts but couldn't act on them
  • Responsive layouts were broken in places and old components were still scattered throughout

What I Shipped

  • Participant drawer — Made scanning easier by adding relevant participant info — skills, work experience, industries — so researchers could quickly understand who they're looking at. Added qualification checks to each screener question so researchers can see at a glance whether a participant qualifies. This made it significantly faster to approve responses and pay incentives, reducing participant wait time
  • Overview & Settings — Fixed the cards, removed unnecessary repeated information, and made key stats clickable for deeper navigation. Fixed the study controls section by adding separate pause buttons for BYOA audience and CleverX panel, making it clear what each action does
  • Screener page — Removed visual noise and aligned styling with CleverX's design language
  • Responsive tables — Fixed how data tables render on smaller screens — kept them scannable without horizontal scrolling or hidden columns
  • Component swap — Pulled in the new component library and removed legacy components across all pages

Methodology

The Audits

The audits weren't limited to these two flows — I went across the full dashboard including billing, settings, and teams pages.

  • Heuristic evaluation — Checked key screens against usability principles (visibility of system status, consistency, error prevention). This is where most hierarchy, labelling, and navigation issues surfaced
  • Usability walkthrough — Went through the product as a user, step by step, noting every friction point and dead end
  • Accessibility audit — Checked contrast, readability, keyboard navigation, and screen reader considerations
  • Competitive analysis — Benchmarked against other research platforms to see how they handle similar screens and information display

Process

How I Worked

This was a new workflow for me, and it changed how I think about my role:

Audit & identify → Design decisions → Claude Code to build → Git push to staging → Team reviews & ships

I owned every design and UX decision. Claude Code was how I got those decisions into working frontend code without waiting in a developer queue. I'd spot an issue, decide what the fix should be from a UX perspective — what information is missing, where it should sit, how it should look — then describe that intent to Claude Code, review the output, and iterate until it matched. Developers reviewed the code before it went live.

The back-and-forth between design thinking and AI is what made it work. I bring the product understanding and the eye — AI removes the bottleneck between decision and execution.

Impact

What Changed

  • Fixed bugs and responsive issues across the dashboard
  • Reduced participant confusion with clear status indicators, next steps, and payment timelines across all study states
  • Made the researcher dashboard actually scannable — key info surfaced, noise removed
  • Unified the visual language by replacing legacy components across every page touched
  • Contributed to a team-wide shift where designers started using AI to ship directly, accelerating overall delivery speed
