CleverX

Designing a Two-Sided User Research Platform

Role UI Designer
Duration 2019 — Present (6 years)
Team 2 Designers + Product & Engineering
CleverX Platform Overview — Hero Image

Overview

CleverX connects businesses with vetted research participants, enabling companies to run user studies, interviews, and surveys efficiently. I've been part of the design team for 6 years, working on the UI across the entire platform.

As one of two designers on the team, I've contributed to designing interfaces for complex workflows, dual dashboards, and billing systems. Product decisions and UX direction are driven collaboratively by our team leads and stakeholders, while my focus has been on translating those decisions into clean, functional interfaces.

The Challenge

Building a two-sided marketplace meant solving for two very different users with competing needs.

Researchers

Needed powerful tools to find the right participants, manage studies, and handle payments — without complexity slowing them down.

Participants

Needed a simple way to discover opportunities, qualify for studies, and get paid reliably for their time and insights.

The core question: How do you create a platform that feels effortless for both audiences while handling complex workflows behind the scenes?

Design Challenges I Worked On

01

Participant Search & Filtering

Researchers need to find specific participant profiles based on demographics, profession, behaviors, and more. The team needed to balance power with usability.

My Contribution

I designed the UI for a progressive disclosure filtering system — showing the most common filters upfront while revealing advanced options when needed. Worked closely with our team leads who defined which filters to include and how they should behave.

02

Study Workflows

Different research methods (surveys, interviews, usability tests) require different workflows. The product team worked out the logic, and we needed interfaces that could handle this complexity.

My Contribution

I designed the interface for the workflow system, creating screens that guide researchers through study setup. The team collaboratively defined the workflow steps and requirements — my role was bringing that to life visually.

03

Dual Dashboard Experience

Some users are both researchers AND participants. The team needed a way to let users switch between these roles seamlessly.

My Contribution

I designed the visual system for both dashboards and the role-switching interface. Created distinct but related visual treatments so users can easily understand which mode they're in while maintaining familiarity across both experiences.

04

Opportunity Discovery

Participants needed a clear way to find and evaluate study opportunities. The product team defined matching logic and what information to display.

My Contribution

I designed the opportunity page layout, focusing on clear visual hierarchy for qualification requirements and incentive information. Worked to make the interface scannable so participants can quickly assess if a study is right for them.

05

Billing & Transactions

Money is sensitive. Both researchers (paying) and participants (receiving) need to trust the system. The team defined what information to show and when.

My Contribution

I designed the billing interfaces for both user types — clear cost breakdowns for researchers and payment status tracking for participants. Focused on making financial information easy to scan and understand at a glance.

Reflection

Being part of CleverX for 6 years has taught me so much about designing for complex products. Working on a two-sided marketplace means every screen affects two different types of users — it's pushed me to think more carefully about visual hierarchy and clarity.

I've learned the value of collaboration. The best interfaces came from close partnership with our team leads, product managers, and developers. Design doesn't happen in isolation — understanding the "why" behind decisions from leadership helped me create better solutions.

I'm grateful to have been part of a team that built something meaningful. Watching the platform grow and knowing I contributed to how it looks and feels has been incredibly rewarding.
